# Sapphire Crystal

The blue is the first thing. Not the pale blue of a winter sky but something deeper, something that ripples with internal light the way sunlight looks from beneath still water. Sapphire formations grow in clusters of hexagonal pillars, each one cold to the touch and faintly humming at a frequency felt more in the chest than heard in the ears. The air around an undisturbed deposit is noticeably cooler and damper than the surrounding cavern. Breath mists here even in summer. Artificers prize the crystal's clarity for focus lenses and mana conduits, but they prize it carefully, because sapphire does not forgive a clumsy hand.

## Harvesting Notes

- Requires a balanced chisel to prevent fracturing the delicate facets. A single misaligned strike can shatter an entire pillar into clouded, unusable pieces.
- Yields 2 sapphire shards per extraction, each requiring careful separation along the crystal's natural hexagonal planes
- Stabilizes nearby water attunement rituals when intact. Removing too much material from a formation can destabilize local hydromantic workings.
- Commonly discovered in flooded caverns, glacial grottos, and the banks of deep underground rivers
- The crystals are slippery when wet, which they almost always are. Secure your footing before cutting.
- Sapphire dust suspended in the air can irritate lungs. A damp cloth over the nose and mouth is standard practice.

## Crafting Applications

- **Focus lenses:** sapphire's optical clarity makes it the preferred material for scrying instruments, telescopic sights, and arcane focusing arrays
- **Mana conduits:** sapphire channels and amplifies water-attuned mana with minimal loss, critical for large-scale hydromantic infrastructure
- **Frost enchantments:** sapphire shards power cold-aspected weapon enchantments and preservative wards for food storage vaults
- **Purification filters:** ground sapphire layered into filtration systems removes toxins and cursed residue from contaminated water supplies

## Lore

Sapphire crystals have long been associated with clarity. Clarity of water, of thought, of purpose. The Hydromancer's College in Thalassport was built directly above a massive sapphire deposit, and its founders claimed the crystal's resonance sharpened their students' concentration and reduced the rate of spell misfires by a measurable degree. Whether this is true or simply institutional pride, the College guards its underground formation with the same intensity that a dragon guards its hoard. They have killed for it. Twice, that anyone knows about.

Divers and deep-cavern explorers tell of sapphire formations found in places no miner has ever reached. Submerged grottos accessible only through underwater passages, where the crystals have grown undisturbed for millennia. These deep sapphires are reported to be almost black in their intensity of blue, and they produce a resonance strong enough to be felt through the water itself. A handful of these stones have surfaced in private collections over the years. Each one commands a price that could purchase a modest estate. The sellers rarely explain how they obtained them, and the buyers rarely ask.
