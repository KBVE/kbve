# Outpost

Stacked stone shaft, timber-framed parapet, crenellated teeth crowning the top, a clan banner snapping in the wind above it all. A torch burns on one side of the roof every night without fail. When the empire wants to claim ground the Capital can't see, the Outpost is how it plants a flag that shoots arrows.

<MapDBPanel data={frontmatter} />

## Game Role

- **Territory emitter** — radius-5 Player territory disc. Can be placed outside existing territory to extend empire reach (one of only two buildings that can do so, alongside the Capital).
- **Arrow volley** — cooldown-gated AoE attack. `OutpostVolley` + `OutpostArrowPool` components drive a 30-second cycle that fires 20 arrows at the closest hostile inside range 15. Burns 5 arrows from the pool per volley.
- **Services** — provides Food (priority 1), Sleep (capacity 10), and Healing (priority 1). Functions as a forward base for patrolling units.
- **Empire connectivity** — joined into the empire territory BFS via `EmpireConnected` once anchored. Outposts outside the connected graph are flagged for the player.
- **Footprint** — single hex. **Does not require existing Player territory** — can be placed on any valid tile to anchor a new claim.
- **Durability** — 220 HP. A serious tactical loss when destroyed; kills the territory emitter.

## Lore

The first Outposts in any campaign are raised by hand in a single day, while the mortar is still setting. Soldiers cut stone, mix lime, and stand watch in shifts. By sundown the tower is tall enough to defend and by sunrise the flag is flying. Speed matters more than finish — an Outpost can be refined after it's already shooting arrows.
