# Capital

Claimed with a Land Grant scroll, the Capital unfurls in a heartbeat — walls, banners, a tile-roofed keep, and a treasury pool all at once. It anchors the empire's first hex, emits a territory radius that lets other buildings plant themselves in its shadow, and feeds every villager who wanders in hungry. Nothing else in the empire exists without it.

## Game Role

- **Founding structure** — placed by the King unit from personal inventory, no construction phase. Spawns fully built and staffed.
- **Footprint** — claims the centre hex plus all six axial neighbours (7-hex flower). Every neighbouring tile is considered part of the Capital for pathing and territory.
- **Territory emitter** — radius-5 Player territory disc so farms, barracks, and production buildings have somewhere legal to plant.
- **Services** — provides Food (priority 1) and Sleep (capacity 10). Hungry / tired units path here by default.
- **Production** — passive recipes (Timber→Plank, Stone→Brick). No tender required.
- **Storage** — stockpiles every item the empire produces through the shared bank ledger. All surplus from other buildings ships here.
- **Durability** — 600 HP, the sturdiest structure in the roster. Repaired by Builders pulling materials from its own stockpile.

## Lore

The Land Grant scrolls predate every living king. Whoever signs one binds an entire civilization's worth of brick, timber, and iron to a single hex on the day the ink dries — an obligation paid forward by centuries of anonymous masons. The scroll is the only acknowledged method for a monarch to begin a kingdom from nothing, and every kingdom's first act is to plant one.
