## Mango

We could use about four different phases of mangos for the recipe; the first form would be the ripe mango, next its fibrous pulp form, then there is puree and finally the nectar.

**Pulp**

Pulp is an important part of the fruit that has juice sacs and fibers, and it can gives the juice more body thus resulting in a nutrient rich thick texture.
Pulp also has some health benefits, such as providing fiber, polyphenols and antioxidants.
The fiber helps with digestion and prevents constipation, while the polyphenols and antioxidants are compounds that fight inflammation and cancer.
But wait! [What are polyphenols](/theory/polyphenols/)?! and what kind do mangos contain?!
Well the mango pulp is rich in polyphenols, especially gallotannins and gallic acid, which are found in the skin and membrane of the fruit cells.
Other minor polyphenols in mango pulp include vanillic, protocatechuic, p-hydroxybenzoic acid, p-coumaric, ferulic, cinnamic, caffeic, chlorogenic acid, mangiferin, and quercetin derivatives.
Source for the polyphenols within mangos come from this research paper, [Mango Mangifera indica L. Polyphenols: Anti-Inflammatory Intestinal Microbial Health Benefits, and Associated Mechanisms of Actions](https://www.mdpi.com/1420-3049/26/9/2732#)

Pulp is a hidden treasure in fruit juice that you should not miss.
But pulp can also cause some problems, such as sedimentation when mixed with whey or affecting the color, flavor and aroma of the juice.
This last part is important to keep in mind when making our juice recipe!

## Preparation Tips & Techniques

When preparing mango juice, the quality of your ingredients and preparation method can significantly impact the final result. Here are some essential tips to elevate your mango juice experience:

### Selecting the Perfect Mango

Choose mangos that yield slightly to gentle pressure but aren't overly soft. The skin should have a fruity aroma at the stem end. Different varieties like Alphonso, Ataulfo, or Tommy Atkins each bring unique flavor profiles to your juice.

### Temperature Considerations

- **Chilled mangos** produce thicker, more refreshing juice
- **Room temperature mangos** blend more easily and release more flavor
- **Frozen mango chunks** create a smoothie-like consistency

### Blending Techniques

Start with lower speeds to break down the fruit, then increase to achieve your desired consistency. Adding a small amount of liquid (water, milk, or coconut water) can help achieve smoother blending.

### Straining Options

- **No straining**: Maximum fiber and nutrients
- **Coarse straining**: Removes large pulp pieces while retaining some texture
- **Fine straining**: Silky smooth juice with minimal pulp

## Mango Lassi

Mango Lassi is a traditional Indian yogurt-based drink that transforms the humble mango into a creamy, probiotic-rich beverage. This cooling drink is perfect for hot days and provides a balance of sweet fruit flavors with tangy yogurt.

### Traditional Mango Lassi Recipe

**Ingredients:**
- 2 large ripe mangos (or 1 cup mango pulp)
- 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
- 1/2 cup whole milk
- 2-3 tablespoons honey or sugar (adjust to taste)
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cardamom
- Pinch of salt
- Ice cubes (optional)
- Chopped pistachios for garnish

**Instructions:**

<Steps>
1. **Prepare the mangos**: Peel and dice the mangos, removing the pit. If using frozen mango, let it thaw slightly.

2. **Blend the base**: In a blender, combine mango pieces, yogurt, and milk. Blend until smooth and creamy.

3. **Add sweetener**: Taste and add honey or sugar as needed. The sweetness should complement the natural mango flavor.

4. **Season**: Add cardamom and a pinch of salt. The cardamom adds an authentic aromatic touch.

5. **Adjust consistency**: Add more milk for a thinner lassi or more yogurt for thickness.

6. **Chill and serve**: Pour over ice or blend with ice cubes for extra coldness. Garnish with chopped pistachios.
</Steps>

### Lassi Variations

**Vegan Mango Lassi**: Replace yogurt with coconut yogurt and use plant-based milk (coconut, almond, or oat milk).

**Protein-Rich Lassi**: Add a scoop of vanilla protein powder for a post-workout treat.

**Spiced Lassi**: Include a pinch of ground ginger, cinnamon, or even a few black pepper granules for complexity.

**Saffron Lassi**: Steep a few saffron strands in warm milk before adding to create a luxurious golden lassi.

### Health Benefits of Mango Lassi

- **Probiotics**: The yogurt provides beneficial bacteria for digestive health
- **Protein**: Greek yogurt adds substantial protein content
- **Vitamins**: Mangos contribute vitamins A, C, and E
- **Hydration**: The high water content helps with hydration
- **Digestive aid**: Traditional Ayurvedic medicine considers lassi beneficial for digestion
