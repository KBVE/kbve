### Hot Wallet

- Internet-connected wallet for easy access to funds
- Suitable for small amounts and frequent transactions
- Higher risk of hacks compared to offline storage
- Ideal for day-to-day crypto use

### Cold Storage

- Keeps funds offline for maximum security
- Not convenient for quick transactions
- Often used for long-term holdings
- Immune to online hacking attempts

### Hardware Wallet

- Stores private keys on a dedicated physical device
- Combines security of cold storage with portability
- Requires connection to a computer or phone to transact
- Considered one of the safest ways to store crypto

### Custodial Exchange

- Third-party service holds your private keys
- Easy to use for trading and conversions
- You rely on the exchange's security measures
- Vulnerable to exchange hacks and policy changes

### Self-Custody

- Complete control over your private keys and coins
- Requires technical knowledge to manage securely
- No third-party counterparty risk
- Responsibility for safeguarding and backing up keys
